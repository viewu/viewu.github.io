Beyond the Books: What a Month of "Small Wins" Taught Me

发表于 2026-03-30 本文字数： 1.5k 阅读时长 ≈ 1 分钟
Summary of March,2026

As the new semester kicks into gear, I’ve been keeping an English diary to track my progress. Looking back at March, I realized that the most important lessons didn’t come from a textbook—they came from real-world “friction.”

1. Owning the “Chain Reaction” ⛓️
I started the month with a travel nightmare—ticketing errors, price differences, and missed verifications. My first instinct was to blame everyone else. But as I calmed down, I realized: What’s done is done. Complaining about small things just drains the energy you need to solve big problems. I fought for my rights, got a partial refund, and moved on.

2. Your Voice is Your Image 🎙️
I had a fascinating realization while dealing with customer service. I saw how a leader’s voice—calm, authoritative, and advanced—can change a situation instantly. It reminded me that communication is a power we must master. Even dealing with a “difficult” teacher is just an opportunity to practice “managing up.” If you can handle a tough leader now, you can handle any boss in the future.

3. Investing in the “High Margin for Error” 📈
As a STEM student, the pressure to find an internship is real. I’ve spent my days balancing algorithm practice with liberal arts reading. I used to worry that reading philosophy was a waste of time for a coder, but I’ve changed my mind.

I’ve also started investing more in myself—like signing up for an off-campus gym and optimizing my workouts with AI. When we are young, our margin for error is higher and the stakes are lower. This is the time to spend our resources on experiences and habits that “upgrade” who we are.

The takeaway? Don’t just stay in the school bubble. Get out, take risks, make mistakes, and find your voice. The price of a mistake today is a bargain compared to the cost of regret tomorrow.
